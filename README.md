# theLook E-Commerce: Marketing Spend Optimization and Channel Risk Analysis

## Business Question
How can we optimize marketing spend to maximize Long Term Value?

## Key Analytical Finding
My analysis concludes that for this specific data, acquisition source is a volume driver, not a behavioral driver. Search users and all other channels exhibit the same spending and retention patterns once they enter the product funnel.

| Metric | Variance Across Channels | Conclusion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **AOV** | < $2.00 Difference | Pricing and basket size are driven by product type, not the ad that brought the user in. |
| **Repeat Rate** | ~20% (All Channels) | Retention is based on product type and less on the marketing funnel. |
| **Revenue** | 70% Concentration (Search) | Massive platform dependency means the business is highly sensitive to Search algorithm changes. |

## Conclusion
The data shows that customers from all channels spend and return at the same rate. Therefore, optimizing marketing spend should focus on buying the highest volume for the lowest price, while shifting budget away from Search to reduce the risk of relying on a single platform for 70% of the company's revenue.
